Slow Fashion

Buy less.Choose well. Make it last. Quality, not quantity. Everybody's buying far too many clothes. Vivienne Westwood

We have come to the point where it is slowly cheaper to buy clothes than food: the massive overproduction of new collections is pouring in every week, and this is what an advertising industry that encourages constant consumption is built. We no longer buy something because we need it, but because we are led to believe that it will make us happier, feel better. Global fast fashion brands do not operate on the principle of durability, but on a low-cost basis: follow trends, keep up, constantly renew.

The problem with this: Cheap clothes are paid for by our environment and labor.

Not only does the creation of materials consume a great deal of water, but thanks to the artificial ingredients, the finished product does not decompose and produces waste, not to mention the tremendous amount of clothes that accumulate. The fashion industry literally cuts the wood beneath itself and adds to global warming and the exhaustion of natural resources. At the other end of the chain, there are workers for pennies who we will never meet, but you should think about the clothes which they sell in the shop for a few thousand, were really made for only a small fraction of the price in a factory.

In contrast, the slow fashion movement has emerged, combining environmental awareness and sustainability.

Slow fashion is a decision that we take responsibility for protecting our environment through our fashion choices.
